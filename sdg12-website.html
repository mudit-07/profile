SDG 12 ‚Äì Responsible Consumption and Production

Sustainable Development Goal 12 aims to ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns. This means using resources wisely, reducing waste, and making choices that protect our planet for future generations.

From the food we eat to the clothes we wear, every choice we make has an impact on Earth. Let's learn how we can consume responsibly and help create a sustainable future!

The Problems We Face

üå≥

Overconsumption of Resources

We are using natural resources faster than the Earth can replenish them. If everyone consumed like high-income countries, we would need multiple Earths to sustain us!

üçî

Food Waste

About one-third of all food produced globally is wasted. That's 1.3 billion tons per year! Meanwhile, millions of people go hungry every day.

ü•§

Plastic Pollution

Over 300 million tons of plastic are produced annually, and much of it ends up in our oceans, harming marine life and entering our food chain.

üè≠

Industrial Waste

Factories and industries generate massive amounts of waste, including toxic chemicals that pollute air, water, and soil, affecting human health and ecosystems.

Important Facts & Information

Understanding the scale of our consumption and production patterns is the first step toward change.

  • By 2050, the global population is expected to reach 9.6 billion people. We would need almost 3 planet Earths to sustain current lifestyles if consumption patterns continue unchanged.
  • Water usage: Globally, 2 billion people live in countries experiencing high water stress. Agriculture alone consumes 70% of all freshwater resources.
  • E-waste crisis: The world generates over 50 million tons of electronic waste annually, and less than 20% is properly recycled.
  • Carbon footprint: The production and consumption of goods account for approximately 60% of global greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Energy efficiency: If the global population adopts energy-efficient appliances and lighting, we could save $120 billion annually in energy costs.

Why does this matter? Unsustainable consumption and production patterns harm our environment, deplete resources, contribute to climate change, and create inequality. By changing our habits today, we can ensure a healthy planet for future generations.
